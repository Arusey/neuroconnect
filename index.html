Who We Are

NeuroConnect is a pioneering organization dedicated to empowering special needs families, schools, and individuals across Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Sudan, and Rwanda. Our mission is to bridge the gap in special needs support by providing structured training, advocacy, and resources to create a more inclusive and informed society.

Our Vision

To be the leading support and training hub for special needs in Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Sudan, and Rwanda, fostering an inclusive community where individuals with special needs can thrive.

Our Mission

  • Providing specialized training for parents, caregivers, schools, and organizations.
  • Creating awareness and advocacy programs to drive inclusion.
  • Offering a platform for networking and resources.
  • Collaborating with experts and policymakers to improve support systems.

What We Offer

Training & Workshops

Structured programs for parents, caregivers, teachers, and organizations to equip them with the skills needed for special needs support.

Advocacy & Awareness

Campaigns, forums, and events to promote inclusion and equal opportunities for individuals with special needs.

Consultancy Services

Guidance for schools and institutions on special needs-friendly policies and infrastructure.

Community Support

A platform where families, educators, and professionals can connect and share resources.

NeuroConnect Club

A vibrant community for individuals, families, and professionals passionate about special needs inclusion and advocacy.

Membership Benefits

  • Exclusive access to workshops, training, and mentorship programs.
  • Networking opportunities with other parents, caregivers, and professionals.
  • Access to expert-led forums and discussions on special needs topics.
  • Participation in social events, awareness campaigns, and community initiatives.
  • Discounts on NeuroConnect services, training sessions, and resources.

Who Can Join?

  • Parents and caregivers of individuals with special needs.
  • Teachers, therapists, and professionals working in the special needs sector.
  • Schools and institutions committed to inclusive education.
  • Anyone passionate about special needs advocacy and inclusion.
